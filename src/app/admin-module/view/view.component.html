DATA CDRs


"Data CDR" typically refers to a "Call Detail Record" associated with data usage, such as mobile data or internet data. A Call Detail Record is a log entry generated by a telecommunications or network service provider to capture information about various types of communications, including data usage.



LOCATION CDRs


A "Location CDR" or "Location Call Detail Record" is a type of record used in telecommunications and network services to capture and log information related to the location of a mobile device or user within a network. Location CDRs provide details about the movement and geographical coordinates of mobile devices or users



SMS CDRs


SMS CDRs, or "Short Message Service Call Detail Records," are records that document details about the exchange of text messages (SMS) on a network. These records are generated by telecommunication service providers to keep track of SMS activities, analyze network usage, and provide a historical record of text messaging events.



MMS CDRs


MMS CDRs, or "Multimedia Messaging Service Call Detail Records," are records generated by telecommunication service providers to capture and document details about the exchange of multimedia messages (MMS) on their networks. MMS allows users to send and receive multimedia content, such as pictures, videos, audio, and text, in addition to traditional SMS (text) messages.



ROAMING CDRs


Roaming Call Detail Records (CDRs) are records generated and maintained by telecommunication service providers when a mobile subscriber makes or receives calls, sends text messages while outside their home network's coverage area. These CDRs are specifically related to activities that occur when a mobile device is in "roaming" mode, which means it is connected to a different network



VOICE CDRs


Voice Call Detail Records (CDRs), often referred to as Voice CDRs or Call Detail Records, are records generated and maintained by telecommunication service providers to keep a detailed account of voice call activities on their networks. These records are used for various purposes, including billing, network optimization, quality assurance, and fraud detection.



VoIP CDRs


VoIP (Voice over Internet Protocol) CDRs, or VoIP Call Detail Records, are records generated by VoIP service providers to track and document details of VoIP call activities. VoIP is a technology that allows voice communication over the internet or IP-based networks, and VoIP CDRs serve a similar purpose to traditional Voice CDRs in telecommunication networks.



SUBSCRIBER CDRs


Subscriber CDRs (Call Detail Records) are records generated by telecommunications service providers to track and document details of various activities related to their subscribers. These records are used for billing, network management, and service quality monitoring. Subscriber CDRs typically contain a wide range of information about the usage of telecommunications services by individual subscribers.
